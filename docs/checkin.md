On track or ahead on platform, product, and migration commitments.

## Progress Against Goals

### GenAI product delivery — On track / ahead
- Delivering RM Assistant and Workbench to production on two-week iterations, rolling out a large number of features since March.
- Delivered the CC Assistant virtual agent and continued post-launch uplift: migrated the vector store to OpenSearch and upgraded to the latest model, while keeping controls and procedures in check.
- Presented RM Assistant at the Merchant Services AI Tech Forum and shared its RAG architecture across teams to spread reusable patterns.

### Platform build (SwarmOps/AgentHub) — In progress
- Building the multi-tenant AI agent orchestration platform on AWS (ECS/Fargate, Aurora PostgreSQL, Bedrock, OpenSearch) with per-tenant IAM isolation, human-in-the-loop approval workflows, and a SQL firewall.

### Infrastructure & resiliency — Delivered
- Led the Lambda→ECS uplift of all 5 micro-frontend apps (UI and backend) in hackathon mode — a major architectural uplift delivered with zero production impact across JPMA, JPMM, and Commerce Center V2.
- Led the multi-region resiliency effort for all MFEs — again a fast-turnaround push, but a well-planned execution, applying lessons from the first uplift to run the second with more structure.

### Migration enablement (Report Center) — On track
- Drove BU/FTI support for Report Center, the work that enables portal migration — completed for JPMA and JPMM in Q1, with Commerce Center V2 targeted for Q3.
- Shared engineering resources with the Commerce Center portal team to accelerate their stories. A strong partnership and a skilled team have kept this effort on track.

### Technology strategy (Turbo evaluation) — Delivered
- Led a six-month evaluation of Turbo, a RAG knowledge-base tool, in partnership with the product and Turbo teams — a thorough assessment of strengths, limitations, and fit against our long-term direction.
- Concluded not to proceed with the tool. The depth of evaluation protected the organization from a misaligned investment and gave the direction we did pursue a well-reasoned foundation — a disciplined "no" backed by significant effort.

### Partnerships & governance — Strong
- Leading the partnership with the Commerce Center portal tech and product teams across production issue triage, feature delivery, and migration readiness.
- Partnering with the CDAO team since their inception as a pilot/early-adopter team — adopting their model management, Phoenix observability, and Evals platforms as they mature, and feeding back real-world input that shapes their progress.

### Release management — On track
- Serving as data release manager for the year, collaborating closely with the release management, testing, and change management teams on schedules, entry/exit criteria, and go/no-go decisions.
